Our Services

Analysis, reporting, testing, and staffing for occupational hearing conservation programs.

Analysis

Audiometric Analysis

Expert review and interpretation of audiometric test results. Each set of results is carefully evaluated to identify hearing threshold shifts, standard threshold shifts (STS), and any changes that may require follow-up or reporting under OSHA standards.

Analysis is performed with attention to both individual employee health and employer compliance obligations.

Identification of OSHA standard threshold shifts (STS)
Year-over-year hearing threshold comparison
Baseline revision recommendations
Referral recommendations when appropriate
Reporting

Reporting

Clear, professional reports prepared for both employers and employees following audiometric testing. Reports are written to meet OSHA recordkeeping requirements and are organized for easy review by HR, safety personnel, and employees.

Individual employee audiogram reports
Employer summary and compliance documentation
OSHA STS notification letters
Workers' Compensation documentation support
Testing

Audiometric Testing

Hearing testing services using properly calibrated audiometric equipment. Testing is conducted following OSHA and CAOHC guidelines to ensure reliable, accurate results that hold up under regulatory review.

Baseline and annual audiometric testing
Testing using calibrated microprocessor audiometers
Onsite and mobile testing options available
Conducted in accordance with OSHA 1910.95 standards
Staffing

Occupational Health Staffing

Qualified personnel available to support your hearing conservation program. Whether you need a CAOHC-certified occupational hearing conservationist for a testing event or ongoing program support, ETCHS can help.

CAOHC-certified technicians
Audiologist oversight and consultation
Flexible staffing for testing events or ongoing programs
Personnel familiar with OSHA Noise Standard requirements
Consultation

Workers' Compensation Consultation

ETCHS maintains current knowledge of Workers' Compensation regulations for noise-induced hearing loss across multiple states. Rhonda provides consultation to employers managing claims and can assist in preparing the documentation needed to support or respond to a hearing loss claim.

State-specific WC rules for noise-induced hearing loss
Documentation review and preparation
Expert consultation for employers and legal teams
Audiometric record review for claim evaluation
